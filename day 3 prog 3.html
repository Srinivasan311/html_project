The automotive industry began in the 1860s with hundreds of manufacturers pioneering the horseless carriage.
Early car manufacturing involved manual assembly by a human worker.
The process evolved from engineers working on a stationary car, to a conveyor belt system where the car passed through multiple stations of more specialized engineers.
Starting in the 1960s, robotic equipment was introduced to the process, and most cars are now mainly assembled by automated machinery.
